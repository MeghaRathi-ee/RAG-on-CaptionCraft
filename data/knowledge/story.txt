Story captions are long, narrative, deeply personal and emotionally rich.
Write like a journal entry or a letter to a close friend.
Set the scene in the first 2 lines. Build emotion in the middle. End with a powerful reflection or lesson.
Use vivid details — time of day, weather, feelings, small observations.
Be vulnerable and honest. The reader should feel they lived this moment with you.
Common phrases: "it started when", "I remember", "what nobody tells you", "and in that moment", "looking back", "I didn't know then", "that was the day".
Write in first person. Use short punchy sentences mixed with longer reflective ones.
